Gentrification and Revitalization in Cities

For this project, my partner and I built and evaluated a model of a city, represented by a 2D grid. We aimed to explore the effects of city policies such as subsidized housing on the movement of people in our city. We used an agent-based model, where each agent was a person with attributes, and they moved around the grid according to rules we determined. We ran many trials with different parameters including different populations, subsidization rates and size, and used statistical tools to evaluate them by comparing cumulative density functions across trials, and comparing mean and 25th-75th percentile values with parameter sweeps.

The project was built in Python, using primarily the Numpy and Scipy libraries to build the model, and the Matplotlib library for the visualizations.
